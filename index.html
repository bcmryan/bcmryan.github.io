Brendan Ryan, Master of Arts Student

My name is Brendan Ryan, and I am a Masters of Arts student in European Linguistics at Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg in Freiburg im Breisgau, Germany with an expected graduate date of summer semester 2017. My studies and personal interests focus on the ever-changing social, political and linguistic landscape of Europe.

I began my studies in Freiburg in winter semester 2015 after graduating from the University of Wisconsin-Madison earlier that year with a Bachelor of Arts with Distinction in German and Linguistics alongside Honors in the Liberal Arts and a certificate in European Studies.

Throughout my studies, I have obtained varying levels of fluency in numerous languages. These include (ranked by level): advanced: German and Turkish; intermediate: French, Spanish and Portuguese; elementary: American Sign Language and Yucatec Maya.

In tandem with learning languages, I have also been involved in numerous research projects focusing on, among other topics, the linguistic analysis of the Kiezdeutsch sociolect of German, the American Indian language Menominee and the Oita-ben dialect of Japanese, as well as linguistics descriptions of Ukrainian and Yoruba. Additionally, I have taken courses focusing on Germanic, Slavic and many other languages and language families.

Alongside my graduate studies, I am currently employed as a research assistant at Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg's German Research Foundation-funded Collaborative Research Centre 948 as a German-English translator and English copy-editor.

In addition to my university studies in Freiburg and Madison, I have also had extensive practical experience on both sides of the Atlantic needed to successfully persue a career in international relations, diplomacy and linguistics.

Beyond structured and independent study courses, I have been able to develop skills needed for a professional, international and public service-oriented working atmosphere as an AmeriCorps member and Community Outreach Coordinator with WISPIRG Energy Service Corps (summer 2012), an intern with the U.S. Department of State at the U.S. Embassy-Berlin (spring 2013) and International Academic Programs at the University of Wisconsin-Madison (2014-15), as an active alumnus (2013-14) and staff (2015-present) of the Academic Year in Freiburg program and as a Study Ambassador for Baden-Württemberg's State Ministry of Science, Research and the Arts (2015-present).

Additionally, I have been an active participant in political events in Germany and the United States and have striven to give back to my host country as an English tutor for under-priviledged children (2013-14) and teacher working with immigrants to Germany (2015-16).
